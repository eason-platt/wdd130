ICE WWR Client Report About Me

Client Report

For my personal website, I decided to commission a website for a mock Formula 4 racing league set locally in Rexburg. I greatly enjoy open-wheeled racing and I thought that this would be an exciting website to have created. The content included has small tips to my own (limited) racing knowledge and the images used are from the real racetracks that were hypothetically recreated. I used other images of real Formula 1 drivers along with the pitlane found at the Monaco Grand Prix. For all of the websites I was a part of, we communicated through Microsoft Teams. Some people attempted to break out and use different communication methods, but that just didn't work. Due to the high workloads everyone experienced during this project, it was much easier to stay inside the dedicated teams channels. Usage of other platforms would also make it impossible for any professor oversight to take place. After the duration of the project, I am very happy with the finished commission. Everything looks incredible and I have no issues with the final product. The developers may have struggled slightly with my wireframe and site plan, so in the future I would most likely add more detail and clearer instructions to the plan.

Rexburg F4

Lead Developer Report

For my lead developer project, things did not go without issues. We were given a disorganized site plan that I suspect was generated by an LLM, as there were URL tracking identifiers pointing to ChatGPT within the wireframe, not to mention it was created completely with ascii characters. This plan did not include much of the necessary content that we needed, and even after being asked to provide said content, we were given nothing. At this point I had to find my own images for the website and then style nearly everything from scratch. Communication was hosted on Microsoft Teams, as previously stated, but in this case one of my junior developers has been missing in action for the full duration of the project. This developer’s ghosting strategy derailed the entire process of developing this website for the client. Outside the intense lack of effort from these two offenders, I was able to host the repository on Github pages like the rest of the websites in this project. I had no issues there. The link to the published site was sent on the teams channel with the client. Developing and managing this project was seamless with Github as well, and that was probably the only part of this experience that went smoothly.

Junior Developer Report

As a junior developer, yet again all communication existed within the dedicated Teams channel. I say that, except one of my team leads forgot to exist for the final project. But then they remembered to exist on the final week after likely vibe coding the commissioner's site plan. Everything was completed and styled so there was nothing for me or the other junior developer to even do. The expectations for these two sites couldn't have been more different. The client who commissioned Earl of Treats communicated terrifically with plenty of content to work with, and the team lead gave us clear and concise directions for the development of that site. I was able to contribute to Earl of Treats, but not the Financial Analytics website. The MIA team lead only assigned child pages to us junior devs after they had fully completed the website which didn’t make any sense. As always, Github performed fine with both of these projects, but that doesn't matter when there isn't anything in the repository to work on.

General Reflections

The most challenging part of this project for me was the inconsistent communication and lack of conscience from multiple group members. This was frankly a massive undertaking in terms of project size at our current level, and I can understand why some people would struggle with the workload. That being said, this did not warrant four week ghosting sessions or defaulting to sorry vibe coding. I do think that I was able to slog through the projects that weren’t progressing normally but only through intense dedication. I did enjoy working with GitHub, though. I’ve interacted with the platform many times before, but I had no idea how developers collaborate and manage their code in real life situations until this project. There are a couple things I would do differently if I worked on this project again. Now that I actually know what it will entail, I would not be stressing nearly as much, and I would make sure to get my work done earlier in the week so that others in the group have more time to work. I do feel like this experience added to my confidence in terms of working with real-world projects because it introduced me to workflows that are similar to those that I would encounter in the workforce. I do wish that the project had even been explained a little bit when we were making our site plans, instead of the modules literally being hidden. The only reason I knew that I would not be developing my own site plan was because I found rubrics for the project explaining that. Please keep the modules published for the sake of student stress levels.
