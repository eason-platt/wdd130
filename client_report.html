ICE WWR Client Developer Project About Me

Client Report

For my personal website, I decided to commission a website for a mock Formula 4 racing league set locally in Rexburg. I greatly enjoy open-wheeled racing and I thought that this would be an exciting website to have created. The content included has small tips to my own (limited) racing knowledge and the images used are from the real racetracks that were hypothetically recreated. I used other images of real Formula 1 drivers along with the pitlane found at the Monaco Grand Prix. For all of the websites I was a part of, we communicated through Microsoft Teams. Some people attempted to break out and use different communication methods, but that just didn't work. Due to the high workloads everyone experienced during this project, it was much easier to stay inside the dedicated teams channels. Usage of other platforms would also make it impossible for any professor oversight to take place. After the duration of the project, I am very happy with the finished commission. Everything looks incredible and I have no issues with the final product. The developers may have struggled slightly with my wireframe and site plan, so in the future I would most likely add more detail and clearer instructions to the plan.

Lead Developer Report

For my lead developer project, things did not go without issues. We were given a nearly useless site plan that was clearly generated by ChatGPT, as there were tracking identifiers pointing directly to ChatGPT within the wireframe, not to mention it was created completely with ascii characters. This souless plan included zero content for us to work with, and even after being asked to provide said content, we were given nothing. At this point I had to find my own images for the website and then style nearly everything from scratch. Communication was hosted on Microsoft Teams, as previously stated, but in this case one of my junior developers has been missing in action for the full duration of the project. They preferred to do absolutely nothing and leave the work for the rest of the team, and we are overworked enough already. This lack of existence from this developer means that one of the pages of the website is lacking any styling aside from the header and footer (done by me obviously) and any content whatsoever. Outside the intense lack of competence from these two offenders, I was able to host the repository on Github pages like the rest of the websites in this project. I had no issues there, and nor did my second junior developer, who worked hard with what he was given. The link to the published site was sent on the teams channel with the client. Developing and managing this project was seamless with Github as well, and that was probably the only part of this experience that went smoothly.

General Reflections

This experience was mostly abhorrent. "Web Fundamentals" including a four week corporate developer larping session involving the creation of three websites was so exciting and fun. The canvas modules explaining what in the world we were even doing were locked until the week of the project, creating a meriod of major issues. Some clients created a website plan having not an inkling of an idea they wouldn't be the ones creating their website. Important content wasn't included in their plans because they had no idea someone else would be developing that website. Nobody was ever instructed to put their assets into zip files and so I had to ask for that every single time, because shocker, you can't take high quality images out of a word document very easily. The one thing I actually enjoyed about this project was seeing my website actually looking good. I wish I had gotten my site plan because it was actually usable, and I wish I was part of that development team because they are capable humans from what I can see. I don't want to do this project again, but if I had to I would not stress as much because I had no idea how little others would be caring about this. Working with llm slop over four weeks doesn't instill much confidence in me either, but the projects that did go well made me feel like I can develop a project successfully... I guess.
